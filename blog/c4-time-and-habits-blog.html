Reflections on Habit and Timeboxing

Which timebox and habits techniques did you try/what worked for you?

I found that a lot of the techniques given in these articles are things that I tend to do, or half-do, already in life. Especially this year I have worked on trying to improve my daily habits. Some of the things mentioned in Fogg’s TEDx talk I have definitely worked into the rhythm of my life. Especially the idea of setting a trigger for yourself after a certain event. After I brush my teeth at night, I put my running clothes next to my bed. After I wake up in the morning, I go running, after I get back from running, I meditate. All of these things chain into each other nicely.

What did you find interesting?

What came up again and again in these articles is the idea of starting small. There is really something magical in the MVP model I think. I don’t know if I would agree with the “floss one tooth” technique. I think that is kind of…unnecessarily small, but I definitely agree with the principle. Like starting with two minutes of meditation, or two minutes of writing each day before bed. 

Which will you experiment further with?

I am going to try find more areas in my life in which I can start small. I design little games a lot, and I definitely have the tendency to get stuck into things long before it is time to do so. Currently I’m making a prototype for a little arcade game where blocks move around a board, and last night I spent an hour drawing these blocks in photoshop instead of coding the AI. A solid grey block would suffice, but I really need the AI to actually make the game playable. It’s just hard to pull yourself away from parts of a project that are fun, to focus on parts that are hard. But, with practice I’ll get there, hopefully.
